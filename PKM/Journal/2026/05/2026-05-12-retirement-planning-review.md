## Decision Framework Insights

The Social Security statement emphasizes:
- Benefits increase approximately 8% per year of delayed claiming from 62 to 70
- Filing strategy affects both personal benefits and surviving spouse benefits
- Working while receiving early benefits reduces amount until full retirement age
- Spousal and survivor benefits may exceed own retirement benefit at full age
- Medicare enrollment is independent of Social Security decision

## Planning Parameters Observed

State comparison shows negligible difference at higher income levels, but Iowa has higher state tax during working years. Idaho tax only applies to earned income, making it slightly more favorable when transitioning to purely investment/benefit income.

Scenarios assume Sam continues working longer than is typical, suggesting delayed retirement claim and full optimization strategy.
