## What I think about here

Strategic decisions around timing of Social Security claiming, tax-efficient withdrawal sequencing, state relocation impact on retirement income, spousal benefit optimization, and timeline for full retirement from work.

Currently modeling three main scenarios:
1. Continue full-time employment through mid-to-late 60s with delayed Social Security claims
2. Transition to part-time work in early 70s while Social Security grows
3. Full retirement with Social Security as primary income source

The decision involves balancing longevity risk, spousal dependency, healthcare transition to Medicare, and tax efficiency across different state income environments.

## Open questions

- What is Diana's estimated retirement benefit on her own record?
- Will spousal benefits or worker benefits be larger for each person?
- What claiming age maximizes household lifetime benefits?
- How much is invested vs in liquid savings for the transition years?
- Are there pension benefits beyond Social Security?
- What are annual healthcare cost estimates during retirement?
- Is relocation to Idaho final, or still being evaluated for tax efficiency?
- What is the target full-retirement-date for complete work cessation?
