New Foundational Decisions — 2026-05-13

Two axes now govern the program. They are correlated but not coincident. Read both together to understand hiring, environment, and workload scope at any point in time.

Maturity tiers (Tier 1 / 2 / 3) describe the team shape — FTE count, coverage posture, governance model. The tier axis is the left column of every comparison in this document.

Implementation phases (Phase 1–4) describe gateway workload coverage — what AI traffic is in scope and what enforcement posture applies. Phases progress through Dev → Test → Prod under their own promotion gates, independently of which tier the team is at. See the Phase Plan section below for the full interaction.

Phase × Tier Interaction
Phase Typical tier Staffing implication
Phase 1 — Discover Tier 1 primary Observe-only posture matches Tier 1 staffing reality. Observability lift (Role 3) is the dominant workload — pull Role 3 forward to 0.5 FTE in late Tier 1 rather than waiting for Tier 2 entry.
Phase 2 — Pareto risk Tier 1 → Tier 2 transition Enforcement on real traffic triggers dedicated AI-security headcount (Role 5 moves consumed → dedicated). Compliance partner becomes embedded. Phase 2 entry is the clearest Tier 1 → Tier 2 trigger.
Phase 3 — Agentic Tier 2 MCP / agent / tool-use expertise is an explicit skill gap — none of Roles 1–13 carry it. Decision needed 3–4 months before Phase 3 entry: net-new hire, upskill Role 1 senior, or contract onramp specialist. See skill-gap callout below.
Phase 4 — Everything else Tier 3 BU-liaison model (Role 13) is the scaling mechanism for onboarding the long tail. Central team focuses on platform health and federation governance.

Skill Gaps Surfaced by the Phase Plan For Nolan — Hiring Sequence
  • Phase 3 — MCP / agent / tool-use expertise. None of Roles 1–13 explicitly carry this. Kong 3.x AI catalog does not yet have first-class MCP-aware audit or agent-session rate-limiting plugins — composition patterns are still being established. Decision needed 3–4 months before Phase 3 entry: (a) net-new senior hire with explicit MCP / agentic-workload experience — cleanest, slowest to source; (b) upskill an existing Role 1 senior with paid training time and an agent-platform mentor — cheapest, riskiest on timeline; (c) contract a Phase 3 onramp specialist for the first 6 months while option (a) or (b) is built — most flexible, highest cost.
  • Phase 1 — Observability-lift weighting. Role 3 (Observability Engineer) is sized for Tier 2 steady state, but Phase 1 is observability-dominant by design. Tier 1 may need to over-index on observability earlier — pull Role 3 forward to 0.5 FTE in late Tier 1, or lean hard on the consumed DXC observability team during Phase 1. Nolan + Pax conversation on Role 3 sourcing recommended.
  • Env-tier coverage in Tier 1. Three Konnect control planes (Dev / Test / Prod) is a 3× multiplier on configuration surface area at Tier 1. Current Tier 1 FTE (1.0 Role 1, 0.5–1.0 Role 2) may be tight. The Tier 1 → Tier 2 trigger of "P1 incident caused by single-point-of-failure" is more likely to fire earlier under the three-env model.

Phase-Transition Triggers — "Move When Any Two Fire"
Tier 1 → Tier 2 Target: 12 months after T1 go-live
Move when any TWO of the following are true:
  • Route count crosses 25 production AI routes
  • Consuming BU count reaches 3 or more
  • P1 incident caused by single-point-of-failure (sole gateway engineer unavailable)
  • On-call burnout documented in a retrospective
  • A compliance requirement mandates 24/7 SLA
  • Token-cost attribution becomes a real budget conversation (not a rounding error)
Tier 2 → Tier 3 Target: 18+ months after T1 go-live
Move when any TWO of the following are true:
  • BU count exceeds 15–20 distinct consuming business units (central onboarding bottleneck)
  • On-call rotation requires >6 FTE to maintain health — signal that embedded BU capacity offloads route-management work better than more central hires
  • Chargeback model has active BU negotiation on SLA customization — BUs want ownership, not service
  • DXC M&A event adds a new organizational entity with a distinct compliance regime
