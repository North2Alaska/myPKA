# Initiating retirement planning specialist hiring

## Context

Jesse came in asking whether he and Diana should work with external expertise on Social Security claiming, Medicare, and tax planning given his FRA status and continued full-time work. The team discovered a gap: none of the current 9 specialists cover retirement/financial planning. Outcome: decided to hire two new specialists through Nolan.

## What we did

- **Larry** mapped team expertise and identified gap in retirement/tax planning.
- **User** clarified scope: two specialists (not three) — one for Retirement/Social Security/Medicare, one for Tax Accountant.
- **Larry** created intake brief document (`Deliverables/2026-05-22-retirement-planning-specialist-intake.md`) with user situation, key decisions, and constraints.
- **User** provided missing data: Jesse's birthdate (03/02/1959, age 67, FRA 01/02/2026), 2025 income ($105,681.16), 401k holdings ($113,927.10), Iowa relocation (June 10, 2026); Diana's birthdate (03/29/1961, age 65), homemaker status, no qualifying income.
- **Larry** updated intake brief with complete situation data.
- **Larry** briefed Nolan to research and draft contracts for both new specialists using SOP-001.

## Decisions made

- **Question:** How many new specialists for retirement planning?  
  **Decision:** Two specialists — Retirement/Social Security/Medicare (comprehensive: claiming strategy, spousal coordination, Medicare enrollment, IRMAA) + Tax Accountant (federal/state tax, capital gains, IRMAA coordination). This keeps both specialists focused while enabling close coordination.

- **Question:** Should we wait for Nolan's hiring brief before gathering situation data?  
  **Decision:** No — gathered complete situation upfront so intake brief is fully populated for specialist onboarding (not a bottleneck on hiring research).

## Insights

- Jesse is at FRA (reached Jan 2, 2026) but still working full-time — this creates complexity around earnings test and Social Security withholding that many retirement advisors underspecialize in. The Retirement specialist hire is specifically for this gap.
- Iowa relocation (June 10, 2026) simplifies tax situation — no state migration tax benefit to chase, but income tax efficiency remains relevant for planning.
